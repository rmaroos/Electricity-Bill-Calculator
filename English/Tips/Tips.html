Electricity Efficiency & Safety Tips

Practical tips for using electricity wisely and safely in your home.

Tips for Efficiency

Use Energy-Efficient Appliances

Invest in appliances that have a high energy efficiency rating to save on electricity costs.

Turn Off Lights When Not in Use

Always switch off lights when leaving a room to reduce unnecessary energy consumption.

Regular Maintenance of Appliances

Ensure that your appliances are well-maintained to operate at maximum efficiency.

Use Energy-Efficient Bulbs

Replace traditional incandescent bulbs with LED or CFL bulbs that consume less energy and last longer.

Install Dimmer Switches

Use dimmer switches to adjust the brightness of lights and save energy by lowering the power consumption.

Cook Efficiently

Use microwave ovens, toaster ovens, or slow cookers instead of traditional ovens when possible, as they consume less energy.

Use Natural Light

Take advantage of natural daylight by keeping curtains open during the day to reduce reliance on artificial lighting.

Safety Precautions

Don't Overload Power Outlets

Avoid plugging too many devices into a single outlet to prevent overheating and fire hazards.

Keep Water Away from Electrical Appliances

Ensure that water and electricity never come into contact to avoid electric shocks.

Never Turn On or Off the Switches with Wet Hands

Never use a wet hand to turn On or Off the switch of the geyser, washing machine, mixer/grinder or any other electrical appliance

Shut Windows Near Plug Points When it Rains

Rain droplets can enter the plug point if the window close to the point is open when it is raining. The droplets can make the plug point vulnerable.

Switch Off Electronic Devices During Thunderstorms

It is good to turn off electrical devices during severe thunderstorms to avoid a fire mishap.

Prevent Exposure to Live Wires and Electrical Parts

Make sure no electrical wires are hanging loose around your house. Keep children out of reach of such wires. This can be fatal.

Remember to Unplug the Appliances After Use

Make sure to unplug the appliances after use. We often forget to unplug our appliances, and this is one of the most dangerous mistakes we make.

Fix Overcurrent Protection Devices like MCB, ELCB, and Fuse

It is advisable to use an ELCB to protect yourself from electrical shocks, particularly during monsoons and in humid environments.
